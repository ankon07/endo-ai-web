What is endometriosis?

Tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus and can cause chronic pain, heavy periods, and fertility issues.

Common warning signs

Severe cramps, chronic pelvic pain, pain during intercourse, painful bowel movements, heavy bleeding, and fertility concerns.

Myths vs facts

Severe period pain is not automatically normal, and pregnancy does not cure endometriosis.

How diagnosis works

Ultrasound can detect some forms such as endometriomas, but definitive diagnosis may still require laparoscopy.

Treatment options

Pain management, hormonal therapy, surgery, and fertility pathways all depend on specialist planning.

Living with the condition

Exercise, heat therapy, anti-inflammatory habits, support groups, and consistent care-team communication matter.
